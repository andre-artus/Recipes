# Traditional-Style Kimchi with South African-Friendly Ingredients

## Description

A traditional-style cabbage kimchi adapted for ingredients that are reasonably easy to source in South Africa. This version uses Chinese cabbage or napa cabbage, daikon or ordinary white radish, spring onions, garlic, ginger, fish sauce, and Korean chilli flakes if available.

For the closest flavour, use **gochugaru**, Korean red pepper flakes. These are often available from Asian supermarkets or online. If unavailable, use a mix of mild chilli flakes, paprika, and a little cayenne.

## Ingredients

### Cabbage

- 1 large Chinese cabbage / napa cabbage, about **1.5–2 kg**
- **140 g** coarse sea salt
- Water, as needed

### Vegetables

- **300 g** daikon, white radish, or Korean radish, julienned
- **100 g** carrot, julienned
- **100 g** spring onions, cut into 4–5 cm pieces

### Rice Porridge

- **10 g** rice flour
- **120 g** water
- **5 g** sugar

### Kimchi Paste

- **35–60 g** gochugaru / Korean red pepper flakes  
  **or** substitute with:
  - **20 g** mild chilli flakes
  - **15 g** paprika
  - **1–2 g** cayenne pepper, optional
- **25 g** garlic cloves, minced or blended
- **15 g** fresh ginger, minced or blended
- **60 g** onion, grated or blended
- **30–45 g** fish sauce
- **15 g** soy sauce
- **5 g** sugar

## Directions

### Salt the Cabbage

1. Cut the cabbage lengthwise into quarters.
2. Trim away the hard core, but leave enough attached so the leaves stay together.
3. Rinse the cabbage under cold water.
4. Sprinkle the **140 g coarse sea salt** between the leaves, focusing especially on the thick white stems.
5. Place the cabbage in a large bowl and leave to salt for **2–3 hours**.
6. Turn the cabbage every **30–45 minutes**.
7. The cabbage is ready when the thick stems bend without snapping.
8. Rinse the cabbage well under cold water **2–3 times** to remove excess salt.
9. Drain cut-side down for **30–60 minutes**.

### Make the Rice Porridge

1. Whisk the **10 g rice flour** and **120 g water** together in a small saucepan.
2. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until thickened and glossy.
3. Stir in the **5 g sugar**.
4. Remove from the heat and allow to cool completely.

### Make the Kimchi Paste

1. In a bowl, combine the cooled rice porridge, gochugaru or chilli substitute, garlic, ginger, onion, fish sauce, soy sauce, and sugar.
2. Mix until a thick red paste forms.
3. Stir in the radish, carrot, and spring onions.

### Season the Cabbage

1. Wear disposable gloves.
2. Spread the kimchi paste between each cabbage leaf, coating the thick white stems especially well.
3. Fold each cabbage quarter into a compact bundle.
4. Pack the cabbage tightly into clean glass jars or a fermentation container.
5. Press down firmly to remove air pockets.
6. Make sure the vegetables are covered by their own liquid.
7. Leave **2–3 cm** of space at the top of each jar.

### Ferment

1. Close the jars loosely, or open them once daily to release pressure.
2. Leave at room temperature, away from direct sunlight, for **1–3 days**.
3. Press the kimchi down once a day with a clean utensil to keep it submerged.
4. When bubbles appear and the kimchi tastes lightly sour, transfer it to the fridge.
5. Refrigerate for at least another **3–5 days** before eating for better flavour.

## Storage

Store in the fridge and keep the kimchi submerged in its brine. It will continue to ferment slowly and should keep for several weeks.

## South African Ingredient Notes

- **Chinese cabbage / napa cabbage**: Often available at larger supermarkets, greengrocers, Asian grocers, or food markets.
- **Daikon / Korean radish**: Use ordinary white radish if daikon is unavailable.
- **Gochugaru**: Best sourced from Korean or Asian supermarkets, or online. Avoid very hot generic chilli powder as a full replacement.
- **Fish sauce**: Thai or Vietnamese fish sauce works well and is usually easier to find than Korean fish sauce.
- **Rice flour**: Available in many supermarkets, health shops, Indian grocers, or Asian grocers.
- **Coarse sea salt**: Use plain salt without iodine or anti-caking agents where possible.
