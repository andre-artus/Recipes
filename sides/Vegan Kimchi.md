# Vegan Kimchi

## Description

Crafted with care, this Vegan Kimchi recipe is a symphony of flavours and a celebration of plant-based goodness. Begin with a fresh head of cabbage, and through a process of salting and resting, watch it transform into the star of this tangy delight.

The vegan sauce, a harmonious blend of soy sauce, coconut sugar, and pineapple juice, adds a delightful twist. The chilli paste, created from ginger, garlic, onion, and chilli flakes, infuses this dish with its signature kick.

As the cabbage undergoes its fermentation journey, your patience is rewarded with a tangy, crisp, and flavourful kimchi that pairs perfectly with a variety of dishes.

## Ingredients

### Vegetables

- 1 head of cabbage, outermost leaves removed and well rinsed
- Sea salt or Himalayan salt, a generous amount
- 3 whole carrots, julienned
- 8 spring onions, roughly chopped
- 8–10 radishes, finely sliced

### Vegan Sauce

- 2 tbsp (30 ml) soy sauce
- 2 tbsp (30 ml) Simple Truth Coconut Sugar, plus more to taste if needed
- ¼ cup (60 ml) pineapple juice
- ¼ cup (60 ml) warm water

### Chilli Paste

- 3 tbsp (45 ml) fresh ginger, peeled
- ⅓ cup (80 ml) garlic cloves, peeled
- 1 small onion
- ⅓ cup (80 ml) chilli flakes, crushed; adjust and increase if needed

## Directions

1. Chop the cabbage into sixths. Remove the bottom core, the hard white section, with a sharp knife and discard.
2. Place the cabbage in a large, sterilised mixing bowl.
3. Pack a generous amount of sea salt between each layer of the leaves by lifting each individual leaf and sprinkling with salt. Repeat until all leaves have been salted.
4. Press down and leave to rest for 45–60 minutes to soften and break down the cabbage, and to draw out moisture.
5. For the vegan sauce, add all sauce ingredients to a small mixing bowl and whisk to combine.
6. For the chilli paste, blend the ginger, garlic, onion, and chilli flakes into a paste.
7. Stir the chilli paste through the vegan sauce.
8. Turn the cabbage over, pat down to compress, and set aside. Repeat this process 3 more times, with a 60-minute resting time between each turn.
9. Mix the carrots, spring onions, radishes, and sauce.
10. Stir to combine, then cover and set aside.
11. Sterilise the glass storage jars.
12. Once the cabbage has been flipped 4 times and has shrunk significantly, rinse and separate each section of cabbage well to remove the salt.
13. Pat with clean kitchen paper to remove excess water and set aside to dry.
14. Wearing disposable gloves, carefully rub the sauce generously all over the cabbage and vegetables, ensuring they are well coated.
15. Pack the vegetables in alternate layers in the jars, patting down to ensure air has been removed.
16. Top with clean, sterilised lids and set in a cool, dark place to ferment.
17. Leave to ferment for 4–6 days. The longer it ferments, the tangier it becomes and the more tender the leaves will be.
18. Each day, open the jars and press the vegetables down with a clean utensil to remove excess air bubbles and ensure the kimchi is always immersed in liquid.
19. A good sign of proper fermentation is seeing little bubbles in the sauce when you press down.

## Storage

The kimchi will keep for 2–3 weeks.

## Note

Ensure all surfaces, utensils, and jars are well sterilised, and that hands are washed throughout the process. This helps prevent introducing bad bacteria and disrupting the fermentation process.
