# Pea Soup

* 3/4 cup split green peas
* 4 cups chicken stock
* 1 stalk celery, chopped
* 1 leek, chopped
* 1 bay leaf
* Thyme
* Garlic
* Parsley
* 

* salt to taste
* ground white pepper to taste
* 1 Yukon Gold potato, cut into 1-inch chunks
* 1 carrot, cut into ½-inch rounds 

* 1/2 Polzka kielbasa (sausage), sliced into ½-inch rounds
* 1-2 Tbsp tomato paste
* 1-2 Tbsp Worcester sauce

1. Combine chicken stock, split peas, celery, leeks, kielbasa, bay leaf, salt, and white pepper in the inner steel pot of pressure cooker.
2. Lock pressure cooker lid in place and set steam vent to Sealing. Select Pressure Cook (Manual) and cook for 20 minutes on High Pressure. Set steam vent handle to Venting to quick-release pressure.
3. Add potatoes and carrots to inner steel pot of pressure cooker.
4. Lock pressure cooker lid in place and set steam vent to Sealing. Select Pressure Cook (Manual) and cook for 10 minutes on High Pressure. Set steam vent handle to Venting to quick-release pressure.

# Smoky Pea Soup (Pressure Cooker or Slow Cooker)

## Ingredients
- Split peas (start with half a packet or less — they expand a lot)
- Water
- 1–2 potatoes, cubed
- 1 carrot, cubed
- Small handful each (when chopped):  
  - Onion  
  - Leek  
  - Celery  
  - Parsley
- 1–2 Tbsp tomato paste
- A dash of Worcestershire sauce
- Salt and pepper to taste
- ¼ tsp ground coriander (or more to taste)
- Optional herbs (your choice)
- Optional: splash of white wine
- **Optional for smoky flavor:**  
  - Small smoked pork hock (eisbein)  
  - Or pre-crisped chopped bacon

## Instructions

1. **Clean the Peas**  
   Rinse the peas thoroughly 2–3 times. Swirl them in a bowl of water or stir with your hands.  
   Drain carefully using a fine colander or strainer. Repeat until the water runs clear.

2. **Soak the Peas**  
   Soak in cold water for several hours or overnight. Drain before cooking.

3. **Cook the Peas**  
   Add soaked peas to a pressure cooker. Cover with water — about 2 inches above the peas.  
   **Do not add salt yet** — it prevents the peas from softening.  
   Cook on low heat for 20–30 minutes until soft.

4. **Prepare Vegetables**  
   While the peas cook, chop your vegetables (onion, leek, celery, parsley, potato, and carrot).  
   You don’t need much — just a handful total when chopped — or more if desired.

5. **Combine**  
   Add chopped vegetables to the softened peas. Then add:
   - Salt and pepper
   - Tomato paste
   - Worcestershire sauce
   - Ground coriander
   - Herbs and wine (optional)  
   Adjust amounts to your taste.

6. **Add Meat (Optional)**  
   For a smoky flavor:
   - Add a small smoked pork hock or eisbein **when adding vegetables**, **or**
   - Add pre-crisped bacon toward the end of cooking  
   
   **Tip:** It's best to pre-cook the pork hock until tender before adding to the soup — this shortens cooking time and avoids tough meat.

7. **Simmer**  
   - Cook over very low heat for about 1 hour, stirring occasionally, **or**
   - Transfer to a crockpot and cook for several hours.
